Lasagna

Description

What is lasagna made of?

Lasagna is a wide, flat sheet of pasta. Lasagna can refer to either the type of noodle or to the typical lasagna dish which is a dish made with several layers of lasagna sheets with sauce and other ingredients, such as meats and cheese, in between the lasagna noodles.

Why is it called lasagna?

The origins of the word lasagne or lasagna can be traced back to Ancient Greece. What we know as lasagne or lasagna is derived from the word “laganon”, which was the first form of pasta. Laganon was a reference to flat sheets of pasta dough cut into thin strips.

Ingredients

  • Wide flat pasta
  • Ragu
  • Bechamel
  • Vegetables
  • Parmeesan cheese

Steps

  1. In a 9x13-inch (23x33 cm) glass baking dish, add a layer of bolognese to the bottom.
  2. Top with noodles, then spread a layer of the ricotta mixture on top.
  3. Repeat with another layer of bolognese, noodles, ricotta, noodles, bolognese, then top with mozzarella and additional Parmesan.
