Okonomiyaki

Description

What is okonomiyaki made of?

It's made with flour, eggs, tempura scraps (tenkasu), cabbage, and pork belly slices and topped with a variety of condiments like okonomiyaki sauce, Japanese mayonnaise, dried seaweed, and dried bonito flakes. If you don't eat pork or prefer another protein choice, this dish is very adaptable.

How does okonomiyaki taste?

Its flavors—sweet and savory, spicy from bits of ginger, smoky with the flavor of dashi—were instantly recognizable (as they would be to anyone who grew up eating casual Japanese food), while the texture, simultaneously crunchy and creamy, hit all of my comfort food sweet spots.

Ingredients

  • Wheat flour batter
  • Cabbage
  • Meat
  • Seafood
  • Okonomiyaki sauce
  • Aonori
  • Katsuobushi
  • Japanese mayonnaise
  • Pickled ginger

Steps

  1. Mix flour and water together in a bowl until smooth. Stir in cabbage, bacon, eggs, sausage, green onions, shrimp, cheese, and tenkasu.
  2. Preheat a griddle to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C) and coat with oil.
  3. Pour 1/4 of batter onto preheated griddle. Cook until golden brown, about 6 minutes per side. Transfer to a serving plate. Repeat to make a total of 4 pancakes.
  4. Make sauce: Mix soy sauce, ketchup, and vinegar together in a small bowl. Drizzle over pancakes.
  5. Garnish with panko and mayonnaise.
